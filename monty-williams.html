    Monty Williams is an American professional basketball head coach and former player, with a net worth of $25 million and a salary of $12.5 million. Monty Williams is the current head coach of the Detroit Pistons in the National Basketball Association. He has been coaching in the league since 2005.

    In June 2023, he became the NBA’s highest-paid coach after signing a six-year $78 million contract with the Detroit Pistons. A month later, he lost his position as the NBA’s highest-paid coach to Gregg Popovich, who signed a five-year $80 million contract with the Spurs.

    Who is Monty Williams?

    Monty Williams was born October 8, 1971, in Fredericksburg, Virginia. Williams played small forward at the University of Notre Dame, where he was named an honorable mention All-American. Throughout his senior year, he averaged 22.4 points and 8.4 rebounds.

    How old is Monty Williams?

    He is currently 52 years old.

    What is Monty Williams’s net worth?

    He is estimated to be worth $25 Million.

    What is Monty Williams’s career?

    Despite a pre-existing cardiac issue that held him out of Notre Dame for two seasons, the New York Knicks selected Monty Williams with the 24th overall pick in the first round of the 1994 NBA Draft. He spent nine years in the league altogether. Williams played with the New York Knicks until 1996, then the San Antonio Spurs from 1996 to 1998, the Denver Nuggets in 1999, the Orlando Magic from 1999 to 2002, and the Philadelphia 76ers from 2002 to 2003. Chronic knee problems eventually forced him to retire. In his NBA career, he averaged 6.3 points, 2.8 rebounds, and 1.2 assists per game.

    Williams was an assistant coach for the Portland Trail Blazers from 2005 to 2010, and his first head coaching appointment came with the New Orleans Hornets/Pelicans in 2010. Williams joined USA Basketball as an assistant coach in 2013, reporting to head coach Mike Krzyzewski. Williams guided the Hornets to the playoffs in 2010-11, placing third in the Southwest Division. The following three seasons, his team finished fifth in the Southwest and missed the playoffs. He was sacked after five seasons with the franchise, having compiled a 173-221 regular season record and going 2-8 in the playoffs.

    In May 2019, the Phoenix Suns signed Williams to a five-year contract. In 2020, he reconnected with star point guard Chris Paul, whom he coached in 2011 while with the Hornets. He won NBA Coach of the Year honors in 2021 and 2022, and again in 2022.

    After leading his squad to the Western Conference’s greatest half-year record, he was selected head coach of the 2022 NBA All-Star Game. The Suns won 64 games this season, setting a franchise record. However, Williams was let go after the Suns were eliminated from the Western Conference playoffs in the second round by the Denver Nuggets. Monty Williams was swiftly signed to a deal to become the head coach of the Detroit Pistons.

    John McEnroe is an American former professional tennis player who was well known for his shooting and volleys, his rivalry with Björn Borg and Jimmy Connors, and his aggressive behavior on the field, often getting him in trouble with referees and authorities.

    In 1979, John McEnroe’s powerful volley helped him win his first US Open and by the end of that year, he was considered one of the best tennis players in the world. He repeated his victories at the US Opens in 1980 and 1981, becoming the first since Bill Tilden to win the title three times in a row and again in 1984.

    John McEnroe ended his career with 77 ATP Tour singles and 77 doubles titles; this is still the highest combined score for men in the open period.

    Tatum O’Neal’s Biography

    Tatum Beatrice O’Neal is an American actress who at the age of 10, became the youngest person to win an Oscar for her role as Addie Loggins in Paper Moon, alongside her father, Ryan O’Neal.

    She later starred in the films The Bad News Bears, Nickelodeon, and Little Darlings, and appeared in guest roles in the television series Sex and the City, 8 Simple Rules, and Law & Order: Criminal Intent.

    Tatum O’Neal was born in the Westwood area of ​​Los Angeles, California to actors Ryan O’Neal and Joanna Moore. Her older brother, Griffin, was born in 1964, and in 1967, her parents divorced and her father soon married actress Leigh Taylor-Young, with whom Tatum’s half-brother was Tatum. They divorced in 1973.

    Tatum O’Neal has a half-brother, Redmond, from Ryan O’Neal’s relationship with actress Farrah Fawcett. Her mother died of lung cancer at the age of 63, following a career in which she appeared in films such as Walk on the Wild Side and Follow That Dream.

    On April 2, 1974, at the age of 10, Tatum O’Neal won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress and the Golden Globe Award for New Star of the Year Actress for her role in Paper Moon, released in May 1973. The youngest person to win an Oscar in the competition, she was 9 years old when filming in the fall of 1972.

    Tatum O’Neal plays Addie Loggins, a child con man mentored by a Depression-era con man played by her father. During a 2010 appearance on RuPaul’s Drag Race, O’Neal said her father did not attend the Oscars with her due to his busy schedule.

    Tatum O’Neal starred in films such as The Bad News Bears (1976) with Walter Matthau, International Velvet (1978) with Christopher Plummer and Anthony Hopkins, and Little Darlings (1980) with Kristy McNichol, and co-starred in Nickelodeon (1976) with her father, and in Circle of Two (1980) with Richard Burton. She appeared as the title character in the Faerie Tale Theatre episode “Goldilocks and the Three Bears” (1984).

    Tatum O’Neal appeared in only five films over the next 15 years, one of which was Basquiat (1996) as Cynthia Kruger. In the early 2000s, O’Neal returned to acting with a guest role in Sex and the City, 8 Simple Rules for Dating My Teen Daughter, and Law & Order: Criminal Intent. In 2005, Tatum O’Neal began playing the recurring role of Maggie Gavin on the long-running television series Firehouse Rescue Me, portraying Tommy Gavin’s boisterous and unbalanced sister, played by Denis Leary.

    In January 2006, she joined the second season of the ABC reality series Dancing with the Stars alongside professional partner Nick Kosovich. They were eliminated in the second round. She then commented on the series on Entertainment Tonight.

    From 2006 to 2007, she played the vengeful and psychotic Blythe Hunter in the MyNetworkTV drama series Wicked Wicked Games. She appeared alongside Nashawn Kearse and Vanessa Williams in the film My Brother (2007).

    In 2008, she appeared in the Lifetime original film Fab Five: The Texas Cheerleader Scandal, based on a true story that happened at McKinney North High School in Texas. She plays the main character’s mother, Brooke Tippit, and befriends the character’s actress, Ashley Benson, with whom she provides acting instruction.

    In 2021, Tatum O’Neal appeared in the film Not To Remember (2021), which aims to raise awareness and raise funds for the fight against Alzheimer’s disease. Directed by Valerio Zanoli, starring Karen Grassle and five Academy Award winners: Tatum O’Neal, Cloris Leachman, Louis Gossett Jr., George Chakiris, and Olympia Dukakis.

    In her 2004 autobiography, A Paper Life, Tatum O’Neal alleged that she was molested by her father’s drug dealer when she was 12, and also alleges physical and emotional abuse by her father, much of which she attributed to drug use.

    She also detailed her heroin addiction and its effects on her relationship with her children. Her father denied the allegations. In a prepared statement, Ryan O’Neal said: “It is a sad day when malicious lies are told in order to become a ‘bestseller.'”

    In 2011, Tatum O’Neal wrote a new collection of memoirs, Found: A Daughter’s Journey Home, which dealt with her tempestuous relationship with her father, volatile marriage to John McEnroe, and recent drug arrests.

    One of Tatum O’Neal’s first public boyfriends was celebrity Michael Jackson, whom she dated in the late 1970s. Jackson described O’Neal as his first love and in an interview with Martin Bashir in 2002, said that O’Neal tried to seduce him, but was terrified of the idea of ​​sex. O’Neal flatly denied any of Jackson’s claims in her 2004 autobiography.

    In 2011, Tatum and her father began to mend their relationship after 25 years. Their process of reunion and reconciliation was captured in the Oprah Winfrey Network short series Ryan and Tatum: The O’Neals House. In 2015, she said she started dating women, choosing not to identify as gay, bisexual, or heterosexual and saying.
